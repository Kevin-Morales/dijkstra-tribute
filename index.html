Edsger W. Dijkstra Tribute

Introduction

Edsger Dijkstra was a mathematician and computer scientist who made contributions regarding concurrency, structured programming, and algorithms pertaining to graphs and control structures.

Dijkstra's Timeline

  • 1930: Dijkstra was born in Rotterdam, Netherlands.
  • 1942: Dijkstra attended Gymnasium Erasminium High School
  • 1945: Dijkstra attended Leiden University to study theoretical physics.
  • 1956: Dikjstra invented the Shortest-Path algorithm for graphs.
  • 1962-1963: Dijkstra invented the semaphore, a data type used for concurrency problems.
  • 1968: Dijkstra wrote the paper, "Goto Considered Harmful."
  • 1972: Dijkstra received the ACM Turing Award.
  • 1974: Dijkstra received the AFIPS Harry Good Memorial Award.
  • 1984: Dijkstra was appointed chair of computer science at the University of Texas, Austin.
  • 2002: Dijkstra died in Nuenen, Netherlands.
